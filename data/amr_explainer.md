# What is Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR)?

Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) happens when bacteria change over time and stop responding to the antibiotics that used to kill them or stop their growth. This means infections become harder — sometimes impossible — to treat.

## Why does this happen?
- Taking antibiotics when they aren't needed (e.g. for viral infections like cold or flu)
- Not completing the full prescribed course
- Taking antibiotics without a doctor's prescription
- Sharing or reusing leftover antibiotics from a previous illness

## Why does it matter?
- Common infections can become life-threatening again
- Surgeries, chemotherapy, and childbirth become riskier without effective antibiotics
- Treatment takes longer, costs more, and may require stronger or more toxic drugs
